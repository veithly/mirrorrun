# Bounty Brief: DevNetwork [AI + ML] Hackathon 2026

## URL
https://devnetwork-ai-ml-hack-2026.devpost.com/

## Deadline
- Official Devpost overview: May 28, 2026 at 10:00 AM PDT.
- Asia/Shanghai equivalent: May 29, 2026 at 01:00.
- Judging: May 28, 2026, 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM PDT on Devpost schedule; AI DevSummit event page also lists sponsor/overall judging on May 28.
- Winners announced: May 28, 2026 afternoon PDT.

## Theme / Track
DevNetwork positions this as a challenge-driven AI and ML hackathon co-located with AI DevSummit 2026, open to in-person and online teams. Teams may build mobile apps, consumer web apps, or business web apps, and may submit to zero, one, or multiple sponsor challenges.

## Prize Structure
- Overall Winner: Amazon Echos for up to 5 team members, DevNetwork Premium All-Access Passes, and announcement to 60,000 subscribers. AI DevSummit lists this as `$12,500 in prize value`; Devpost overview lists `$8,000+ in prizes`.
- Crusoe: Build a Hermes / NemoClaw agent running Nvidia Nemotron on Crusoe Cloud Managed Inference. 1st: NVIDIA DGX Spark. 2nd: Mac mini.
- Perfect Corp: Building the next generation of AI-driven consumer experiences. 1st: $1,500. 2nd: $1,000.
- TrueFoundry: Resilient Agents. Devpost overview lists 1st: $1,000 and 2nd: $500; the sponsor detail page lists 1st: $500 and 2nd: $200. Treat this as inconsistent and optimize for the challenge anyway because it overlaps with production-readiness.
- Lark: Best use of Lark CLI and/or MCP. 1st: $1,000. 2nd: $500. Top 5 submissions may be featured on Lark social accounts.

## Rubric
Overall judging:
- Progress: How much progress did the team make?
- Concept: Does the submission solve a real problem?
- Feasibility: Could the submission grow into a startup or profitable business?

Sponsor judging:
- Each sponsor judges its own challenge and chooses its own winner.
- Multiple challenge submission is allowed.

## Required Stack / Sponsor APIs
- Crusoe Managed Inference with a Hermes / NemoClaw agent using Nvidia Nemotron.
- Perfect Corp YouCam APIs for at least one AI/AR consumer or retail feature.
- TrueFoundry AI Gateway or related platform surface for resilient agent behavior under LLM/MCP failure.
- Lark CLI and/or MCP for useful developer workflow tooling.

## Strongest Prize Strategy
Build one product that has:
- a visible consumer retail experience for Perfect Corp,
- a real Nemotron agent backbone for Crusoe,
- a chaos/failover proof surface for TrueFoundry,
- a developer workflow and replay automation surface for Lark,
- a business story around launching reliable AI retail experiences.

## Forbidden Patterns
- Thin chatbot around sponsor APIs.
- Sponsor-logo checklist where tools are not structurally necessary.
- Static dashboard with no real user action.
- Fake try-on results, fake Lark run ids, fake provider failure, or hard-coded model receipts.
- Submission copy that opens with stack names instead of a user problem.
- Any public UI copy saying "hackathon", "MVP", "prototype", "demo wallet", or model brand names as the main value.

## Submission Form Fields
From the published Google instructions, project creation requires:
- Project name.
- Elevator pitch.
- Created by / team members.
- Whole story.

Recommended before deadline:
- Built With.
- Image gallery screenshots.
- Try It Out URL.
- Video demo.

Submission step:
- Select applicable sponsor challenges.
- Accept terms.
- Submit to hackathon.

## Delivery Mode
`combined-pitch-demo`

Reason: public Devpost instructions call for a video demo but do not list a separate slide-deck field. The safest package is one continuous 4-5 minute video that includes pitch chapters and demo chapters, with a 1-3 minute Perfect Corp-compatible cut if needed.
